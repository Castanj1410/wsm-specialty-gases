Frequently Asked Questions

How many components can WSM put in a single mixture?

+
WSM manufactures gas mixtures from 2 components (binary) up to 12 or more components in a single cylinder. Each component is independently verified by analytical measurement. Contact us with your full specification and we'll confirm feasibility and lead time.

What is an EPA Protocol Gas?

+
EPA Protocol gases are calibration gas standards manufactured based on the EPA "Traceability Protocol for Assay and Certification of Gaseous Calibration Standards" (Document EPA/600/R-12/531, May 2012). This protocol establishes the analytical and statistical procedures for certifying gases used to calibrate Continuous Emissions Monitoring Systems (CEMS) under 40 CFR Part 72 and the Clean Air Act. WSM is a participant in the EPA Protocol Gas Verification Program (PGVP) — our protocol gases are NIST-traceable with expanded uncertainty stated at k=2.

What makes sulfur gas mixtures different?

+
Sulfur compounds (H₂S, SO₂, COS, mercaptans) are reactive and can adsorb onto cylinder walls, causing concentration loss over time. WSM uses aluminum cylinders with a proprietary treatment to create a stable internal surface. This ensures the labeled concentration remains accurate throughout the full shelf life of the cylinder.

What is the turnaround time for custom mixtures?

+
Turnaround depends on the complexity of your mixture, but we have mixtures that can ship as fast as 3-7 business days. Contact us for more information based on your specific requirements.

Does WSM provide medical gas mixtures?

+
Yes. WSM manufactures medical gas mixtures to USP and cGMP standards including lung diffusion (DLCO) test gas, blood gas calibration mixtures, aerobic and anaerobic culture gas, and anesthesia verification standards. Full documentation for regulatory compliance and FDA certification available.

What does NIST-traceable mean?

+
NIST-traceable means the calibration of our analytical instruments and reference standards can be traced through an unbroken chain of comparisons back to a NIST Standard Reference Material (SRM). As an ISO 17034 accredited Reference Material Producer, WSM documents this full traceability chain and reports the expanded measurement uncertainty at k=2 (95% confidence level).
