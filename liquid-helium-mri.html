Applications

Where HeliStream® Helium Is Mission-Critical

Helium's extremely low boiling point (-269°C / -452°F) makes it indispensable for applications requiring cryogenic cooling that no other substance can achieve.

🏥

MRI Magnet Cooling

Liquid helium maintains the superconducting state of MRI magnets at 4.2K (−269°C). Essential for hospitals, imaging centers, and mobile MRI units. Magnet capacities vary between 300 and 2,300 liters, requiring periodic refills to maintain optimal helium levels.

🔬

NMR Spectroscopy

Nuclear Magnetic Resonance spectrometers require liquid helium for superconducting magnet operation. Critical for pharmaceutical research, university chemistry departments, and materials science laboratories worldwide.

🚀

Aerospace & Cryogenics

Rocket fuel pressurization, satellite testing, superconductor research, particle physics experiments, and quantum computing systems — all require ultra-pure helium supply at cryogenic temperatures.

💻

Semiconductor & Quantum

Cooling for quantum computing dilution refrigerators, semiconductor epitaxial growth chambers, and advanced materials research. Every 1 MW of quantum processing requires approximately 12,000 liters of helium annually.
